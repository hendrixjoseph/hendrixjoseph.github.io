This was the *easiest* cake request so far. So much easier than [a Mario cake](https://www.joehxblog.com/a-mario-cake-for-my-sons-birthday/), [a Rapunzel cake](https://www.joehxblog.com/a-rapunzel-cake-for-my-daughters-birthday/), [an Ariel cake](https://www.joehxblog.com/an-ariel-cake-for-my-daughters-birthday/), [a penguin cake]( https://www.joehxblog.com/a-penguin-cake-for-my-sons-birthday/), or [an elephant]( https://www.joehxblog.com/an-elephant-cake-for-my-sons-birthday/).

After all, a poké ball is just... a ball. That is round. And round, circle cake pans are pretty standard.

I also used strawberry icing for the red parts of the poké ball. Yes, it should be more red than pink, but from my experiences with the Mario cakes, it takes a *lot* of red food coloring to make red icing - which can be messy.

The black part of the poké ball - which is more outline than black - I used some black decorative icing I had on hand.

Was my some happy with the result? Absolutely. He was thrilled.
